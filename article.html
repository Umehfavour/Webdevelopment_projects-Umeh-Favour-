Breaking the Barrier

For decades, the technology industry has been dominated by men. From Silicon Valley boardrooms to Lagos startup hubs, women have faced structural barriers — from unequal access to education, to unconscious bias in hiring. But that narrative is rapidly changing, driven by communities, mentorship programs, and sheer determination.

Communities like She Code Africa, Women Techmakers, and AnitaB.org are creating pipelines of skilled women engineers, data scientists, and product managers who are not just joining the industry — they are leading it.

What Comes Next

The road ahead requires continued investment — in scholarships, in mentorship, and in creating workplaces where women are not just welcomed but genuinely empowered. The next generation of African women in tech is already learning to code, building startups, and earning certifications that put them on the global stage.

The question is no longer whether women belong in tech. The question is how fast we can remove the remaining barriers so they can lead it.
